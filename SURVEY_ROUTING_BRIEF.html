Utah REIA — Internal Tech Brief

Survey Submission Routing System

When a member submits a readiness survey in GHL, this system automatically qualifies them, scores their priority level, routes their record into Airtable, and notifies the admin team — all without any manual work.
Date: April 14, 2026 Status: Live & Running Covers: 7 Surveys

Overview

Utah REIA uses readiness surveys to understand where members are in their investing journey. Every time someone submits one of these surveys, a fully automated system runs in the background to:

  • Identify who the person is by looking them up in GHL
  • Check whether they are an active paying member
  • Record their calculator interest (BRRRR, Fix & Flip, Wholesale, etc.)
  • Score their priority level so the admin team knows who to follow up with first
  • Save everything to Airtable automatically
  • Notify the admin team instantly
The bottom line: The moment someone submits a survey, Utah REIA knows who they are, what they want, how serious they are, and whether they should be approved — before anyone on the team has looked at it.

How It Works — Full Chain

GHL
Member submits a readiness survey in GHL (e.g. BRRRR Readiness Survey)
GHL
GHL workflow fires a webhook to the Vercel API, sending the member's name, email, phone, and survey name
VERCEL
The /api/survey-deal-access endpoint receives the submission and begins processing
VERCEL
GHL contact lookup: searches GHL by phone or email to find the contact record and confirm they exist in the CRM
VERCEL
Airtable lookup: checks if this person already has a record in "Deal Access Requests". If yes, updates it. If no, creates a new one.
VERCEL
Interest tracking: adds the new calculator type to their list of interests — previous interests are never erased, they stack over time
VERCEL
Priority scoring: calculates a 0–100 score based on membership status, engagement depth, and whether this is their first submission
VERCEL
Access status: active members are automatically approved. Everyone else is marked Pending for admin review.
AIRTABLE
The full record is saved to the "Deal Access Requests" table in Airtable with all fields populated
ADMIN
An instant notification fires to the admin team with a summary of the submission, priority score, and access decision

How Priority Scoring Works

Every submission receives a score from 0 to 100. This score tells the admin team who needs attention first — without anyone having to manually review every submission.

How Points Are Earned

+40
Member Status = "Active" in Airtable
+20
Known contact already in GHL
+10
Per calculator interest (up to 5)
+10
First ever submission — fresh lead

Priority Levels

HIGH
Score 60 or above
Active member, engaged, or has multiple interests. Follow up immediately.
MEDIUM
Score 30 – 59
Known contact with some engagement. Worth nurturing soon.
LOW
Score below 30
New or unknown contact. Add to nurture sequence and monitor.
Real example: A member submits the BRRRR Readiness Survey. They are an active member (+40), already in GHL (+20), and this is their 3rd calculator interest (+30). Their score = 90 → High Priority. They are automatically approved and the admin team is notified immediately.

Automatic Access Decisions

The system makes an access decision on every submission — no manual review required for active members.

Who SubmittedAccess Status SetWhat Happens Next
Active paying member Approved Automatically approved. Admin is notified. No action needed.
Known GHL contact (not active member) Pending Saved to Airtable for admin review. Admin is notified.
New contact (not yet in GHL) Pending New record created. Admin is notified. Team can follow up.
Previously reviewed (manual decision) Kept as-is The system never overwrites a decision already made by the admin team.

Multi-Interest Tracking

If a member submits multiple surveys over time — for example the BRRRR survey today and the Fix & Flip survey next month — the system stacks their interests rather than replacing them.

Example — Same member, three surveys over 6 months:

January  → BRRRR Readiness Survey      Interests recorded: [BRRRR]
March   → Fix & Flip Readiness Survey   Interests recorded: [BRRRR, Fix & Flip]
June    → Wholesale Readiness Survey    Interests recorded: [BRRRR, Fix & Flip, Wholesale]

Their score also increases with each new interest, so they move from Medium to High priority over time.

What Gets Saved to Airtable

Every submission creates or updates a record in the "Deal Access Requests" table with these fields:

Name
Auto-capitalised from the survey submission
Email
Stored in lowercase for consistency
Phone Number
Normalised to 10-digit format
GHL Contact ID
Links the Airtable record directly to their GHL contact
Survey Name
The exact survey they submitted
Calculator Type
Resolved from the survey name (e.g. "BRRRR")
Calculator Interests
Full list of all strategies they have ever shown interest in
Request Date
Date of the most recent survey submission
Access Status
Approved or Pending — never overwritten once manually set
Priority Score
0–100 numeric score for sorting and filtering
Priority Level
High / Medium / Low — easy at-a-glance view
Notes
Any additional notes passed in with the submission

Configuration — What Needs to Be Set Up

The system runs on environment variables stored in Vercel. These need to be confirmed as active:

VariableWhat It DoesRequired?
AIRTABLE_API_KEY_DEAL_ACCESS Authenticates with Airtable to read/write records Required
AIRTABLE_BASE_ID_DEAL_ACCESS Points to the correct Airtable base Required
AIRTABLE_TABLE_DEAL_ACCESS Table name (defaults to "Deal Access Requests" if not set) Optional
GHL_API_KEY Enables GHL contact lookup and active member check Optional but recommended
GHL_LOCATION_ID GHL sub-account ID — required alongside GHL_API_KEY Optional but recommended
Member Status (Airtable field) Active member check reads this Airtable field — value must be "Active" or "Inactive". GHL tags are not used. Required for auto-approval
ADMIN_NOTIFY_WEBHOOK_URL GHL webhook URL that receives the admin notification on every submission Optional but recommended
GHL_WEBHOOK_SECRET Security key to verify that requests are genuinely coming from GHL Optional

What This Enables Next

Now that every survey submission is captured and scored, the next logical step is to use that data to send the right follow-up automatically — routing people to resources that match their specific need.

Survey SubmittedSuggested Follow-Up Routing
BRRRR Readiness Survey Send refinance resources, vetted contractors, BRRRR education
Fix & Flip Readiness Survey Send rehab estimator, contractor resources, hard money lenders
Rental Readiness Survey Send property management resources, long-term financing guides
Short-Term Rental Readiness Survey Send Airbnb/STR setup guides, furnishing resources
Wholesaler Readiness Survey Send deal-finding resources, contract templates, buyer's list info
Your Path to Success Send general onboarding, next steps, upcoming events
This routing is already partially in place. The BRRRR calculator phone-gate automation (built separately) already fires when a member accesses the calculator. Survey-based routing would extend the same logic to trigger from the survey submission instead — reaching members even before they open a calculator.
